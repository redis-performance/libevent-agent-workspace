### Chair Decision

**Winner**: single variant  
**Hypothesis**: Deferring `EPOLL_CTL_DEL` in `epoll_nochangelist_del` and converting the next `EPOLL_CTL_ADD` for the same fd into `EPOLL_CTL_MOD` will reduce the epoll_ctl syscall count from 200 to 100 per `run_once()` setup iteration in cascade_bench, reducing median µs by approximately 8–15%.  
**Runner-up**: none evaluated  
**Park for later**: Tier 1a (chain freelist) — not exercised by cascade workloads

## Decision

**Status**: REJECT  
**Reason**: The optimization saves `epoll_ctl` syscalls exclusively in the untimed setup phase. cascade_bench times only the dispatch loop (gettimeofday wraps the cascade do-while, not the event_del+event_add loop). cascade_chain times the event registration (100 ADD) and dispatch but NOT the DEL-at-teardown. Net measured impact: 0% on cascade_bench, +1.6% on cascade_chain (within noise, stddev≈40µs).

## Lessons

1. **Read the benchmark timing window before hypothesizing.** cascade_bench measures only the cascade dispatch loop, NOT the setup (event_del+event_add). The `gettimeofday(&ts)` is called AFTER the registration loop and the first EVLOOP_ONCE drain. All epoll_ctl from setup are outside the timed window.

2. **cascade_chain times registration (ADD) but not teardown (DEL).** The ADD calls (100 epoll_ctl) are in the timed window; the DEL+close loop after `gettimeofday(&te)` is not. So optimizing the DEL path gives zero measured benefit.

3. **Next targets for these benchmarks**: The timed hot path is the dispatch loop — `event_base_loop` overhead per iteration (Tier 4/5), `evmap_io_active_` activation path, and `event_process_active_single_queue`. For cascade_chain, the 100 `EPOLL_CTL_ADD` calls (in the timed window) are also a target.
